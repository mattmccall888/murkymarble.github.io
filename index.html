The Murky Marble

  • In the vast cosmos, Earth spins with grace,
  • A blue gem shining in endless space,
  • Nature’s bounty in a gentle embrace,
  • Yet hidden perils we must face.
  • Waters once vibrant now bear a toxic hue,
  • Rivers and streams turn murky from residue,
  • Industrial spills and runoff taint the blue,
  • A plea for cleansing, for waters pure and true.
  • Forests lush and green are losing their embrace,
  • Mighty trees felled, leaving an empty space,
  • The whisper of leaves silenced without a trace,
  • Nature weeps over her vanishing grace.
  • Skies once clear now drown in choking smog,
  • Air heavy with toxins—a relentless clog,
  • Breaths turn shallow under a chemical fog,
  • A warning call echoed by the urban cog.
  • Rise, restore, let nature reclaim her art,
  • Heal the wounds and mend each broken part,
  • Together, ignite change with a hopeful heart,
  • For Earth is our home—a precious start.
